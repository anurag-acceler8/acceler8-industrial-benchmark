Work-Audit Survey Methodology

How We Build Your Work-Audit Survey

One tailored survey per role — built from real evidence about your organisation, not a generic template.

1What informs each survey

  • Role — the distinct role identified from your job titles during the earlier role-grouping step.
  • Job architecture — the validated view of the work expected for the role.
  • Job description — your view of the role's responsibilities and technical skills.
  • Occupation baseline (O*NET) — reference information for comparable occupations.
  • Strategy context — shapes emphasis and wording and may inform a small number of strategic alignment questions.
How we use evidence

We use all available sources together. Where sources conflict, the job architecture and job description resolve the role's expected work. Strategy context may shape emphasis, wording and a small number of strategic alignment questions, but it does not define the activity backbone.

2How we turn that into questions

1 Start with how time is spentMap a typical week across the role's activities.
2 Add the measures that matterAssess flow, delivery, tools & AI, capacity and how the work may evolve.
3 Tailor questions to the roleInclude each measure only where relevant — for example, coding questions are for hands-on engineers, not managers.

What we measure

Time & hours
Time spent across the role's activities.
Flow & focus
Interruptions, focus time and barriers.
Delivery & output
Volume and turnaround time, where relevant.
Tools & AI
Tools used and the impact of AI.
Capacity & knowledge risk
Workload, available capacity and reliance on key individuals.
Work evolution (1–2 years)
Activities likely to grow, decline or be automated.
Every question stands aloneClear opt-out options prevent forced or inaccurate answers.
Role-specific surveysEach role receives questions tailored to its work and language.
Employee and manager wordingEvery question includes matched wording for employees and managers.
