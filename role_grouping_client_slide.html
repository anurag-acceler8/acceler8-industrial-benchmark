How We Grouped Your Roles — Technology Service

22 job titles reviewed → 14 unique roles — so each person receives one relevant survey, with no duplicates. Grouping uses structured signals and evidence, not job titles or seniority alone.

What we group on

  • Same occupation familyRoles mapping to the same standard occupation are candidates to be one role.
  • Same manager / job ladderSame team and reporting line points to one role at different levels.
  • Near-identical title (level words removed)Strip L1/L2/Senior/Lead/Trainee → level variants → one survey.
  • Task overlap in job descriptions~70%+ overlap in day-to-day work means the same role.
  • Client confirmation on borderlinePartial overlap (~40–70%) → we ask you rather than guess.
  • Hard rulesExecutives standalone; managers never merged with hands-on roles; titles/O*NET alone never enough; no JD = provisional only.
  • Evidence orderJD tasks ≈ client answers > org structure > titles / O*NET.
